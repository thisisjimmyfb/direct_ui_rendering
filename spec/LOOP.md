## Pending Tasks
- Fix shadow acne on back wall: add constant + slope-scale depth bias to `room.frag` PCF — In `sampleShadowPCF`, receive the interpolated surface normal and light direction from the vertex shader, compute `bias = max(0.005 * (1.0 - dot(N, L)), 0.001)`, and subtract it from `proj.z` before the `sampler2DShadow` comparison. Update `room.vert` to output `outNormal` already in use and pass `lightDir` from the UBO. This is the primary fix for the self-shadowing caused by floating-point precision differences between the shadow pass and main pass; slope-scaling ensures the back wall (low N·L) gets larger bias than the floor.
- Fix PCF kernel asymmetry in `room.frag` — Change the 2×2 PCF loop from `x/y ∈ {0,1}` to centered offsets `{-0.5, 0.5}` in both axes. The current filter is offset entirely to the positive direction, creating directional shadow-edge artifacts. A centered kernel produces symmetric soft shadows.
- Tighten light orthographic frustum in `scene.cpp::lightViewProj()` — Replace `glm::ortho(-5,5,-5,5, 0.1, 30)` with bounds derived from the actual room extents (room is ±2 m wide, 0–3 m tall, ±3 m deep; light camera at 10 m from origin). Current frustum wastes ~75% of the 1024×1024 shadow map on empty space, reducing texel density on walls and worsening depth precision on the back wall.
- Add render test: back wall not self-shadowed — In `test_containment.cpp` (or a new `test_shadow.cpp`), add a headless render test that positions the camera facing the back wall, renders one frame, reads back the back wall region (~centre strip), and asserts that the mean luminance of those pixels exceeds `ambientColor + 0.1` (proving that at least some diffuse light contribution reaches the wall, i.e., the depth bias prevents total self-shadowing).
